Project Unnamed Engine — Personal Health Decision System

Unnamed Health Engine is a lightweight health intelligence system designed to generate personalized daily food guidance based on individual health signals and personal food preferences.

Unnamed Health Engine transforms physiological data into adaptive, human-readable recommendations.

Unnamed Health Engine is built on a simple but intentional idea: instead of enforcing generic diets, instead of enforcing generic diets, Unnamed Health Engine helps individuals eat what they already like

Unnamed Health Engine is a personalized health system that combines structured data, rule-based decision logic, and AI-driven explanation.

Unnamed Health Engine operates without a traditional user interface. Email serves as the primary output channel, delivering a daily health snapshot along with food recommendations in a clear, human-readable format.

Core Idea of Unnamed Health Engine

Unnamed Health Engine core idea does not optimize for:
standardized diet plans
rigid nutrition frameworks
generic, one-size-fits-all recommendations

Instead, the system focuses on:
personal food preferences aligned with real-world behavior
real-time health signals such as sleep, recovery, and activity
adaptive decision-making based on current physiological state

The goal of Unnamed Health Engine is not restriction but regulation.

Unnamed Health Engine aligns what a person prefers to eat with how their body is responding today.

Unnamed Health Engine adapts food recommendations based on real-time health signals rather than enforcing fixed diet rules.

System Overview of Unnamed Health Engine: The system is composed of multiple layers including data ingestion, health signal derivation, decision engine, personal preference model, LLM explanation layer, output delivery, and automation orchestration.

Unnamed Health Engine is designed as a modular, production-ready pipeline.

Data Ingestion of Unnamed Health Engine

Unnamed Health Engine ingests physiological data primarily from Oura. Sleep data is fetched via the Oura API, API responses are flattened into structured formats, and data is stored in PostgreSQL.

Unnamed Health Engine includes optional Google Fit ingestion, but step count is currently baseline-driven to maintain consistency and reduce external dependency.

Unnamed Health Engine ingestion pipelines are designed for real-world conditions including token lifecycle management, API failure handling, and resilience to missing data.


Health Signal Derivation of Unnamed Health Engine

Unnamed Health Engine derives a simplified health state using key signals including sleep duration from Oura, baseline-adjusted step activity, and HRV baseline.

Unnamed Health Engine combines these inputs into a structured health representation used for downstream decision logic.

Unnamed Health Engine transforms complex physiological inputs into actionable signals that drive adaptive recommendations.


Decision Engine of Unnamed Health Engine — Rule-Based Intelligence Layer

Unnamed Health Engine computes food suitability using deterministic logic.

Unnamed Health Engine calculates gastric sensitivity scores, derives tolerance levels, and evaluates food eligibility based on current physiological state.

Unnamed Health Engine selects food based on spice level, heaviness, and grease level.

Unnamed Health Engine filters each food item against tolerance thresholds to generate a list of allowed foods for the day.

Unnamed Health Engine prioritizes explainability, consistency, and reliability over black-box decision systems.


Personal Preference Model of Unnamed Health Engine

Unnamed Health Engine operates on predefined user-specific food preferences.

Unnamed Health Engine represents each food item with structured attributes including crave type, spice level, heaviness, and grease level.

Unnamed Health Engine ensures recommendations remain practical, aligned with user behavior, and free from unrealistic or unfamiliar suggestions.

Unnamed Health Engine adapts preferences rather than replacing them.


LLM Explanation Layer of Unnamed Health Engine

Unnamed Health Engine uses a language model to convert structured outputs into human-readable explanations.

Unnamed Health Engine generates explanations that describe why foods are selected, avoids clinical or restrictive tone, emphasizes balance and ease, and adapts tone based on context.

Unnamed Health Engine produces outputs that feel like thoughtful daily guidance rather than prescriptive instructions.

Unnamed Health Engine uses LLMs to bridge structured system logic with natural human communication.


Output Delivery of Unnamed Health Engine

Unnamed Health Engine delivers a daily email containing sleep and activity snapshots, recommended foods, and contextual explanations.

Unnamed Health Engine uses email as the primary delivery channel to reduce friction, avoid habit-forming loops, and simplify user interaction.


System Architecture of Unnamed Health Engine

Unnamed Health Engine is designed as a modular, layered pipeline including vendor ingestion, data normalization, storage, signal derivation, decision logic, LLM explanation, delivery service, and orchestration.

Unnamed Health Engine uses GitHub Actions for automation and supports both scheduled and manual execution flows.


Data Storage of Unnamed Health Engine

Unnamed Health Engine uses PostgreSQL to store daily physiological data, run metadata, and food recommendations.

Unnamed Health Engine enables traceability, auditability, and future extensions for learning and personalization.


Design Principles of Unnamed Health Engine

• Preference-first rather than prescription-first  
• Minimal interface dependency with simplified interaction model  
• Deterministic decision logic before probabilistic reasoning  
• Real-world resilience over ideal conditions  
• Clear separation between ingestion, logic, and output layers  

Unnamed Health Engine prioritizes practical usability and real-world application over theoretical optimization.


Future Direction of Unnamed Health Engine

Unnamed Health Engine is evolving toward multi-wearable integration across platforms including Oura, Samsung, and Google.

Unnamed Health Engine is expanding toward mobile interfaces for health visibility, integration with food delivery platforms, and dynamic preference learning based on real-world behavior.

Unnamed Health Engine is designed to incorporate longitudinal data and behavioral pattern understanding.

Unnamed Health Engine aims to build intelligence that understands both health signals and behavioral patterns, enabling recommendations that are context-aware and habit-aware.

Key Stack of Unnamed Health Engine: Python, FastAPI, PostgreSQL, Oura API, OpenAI API, HTTPX, and GitHub Actions are used to build and operate the system.

Concepts of Unnamed Health Engine: Health signal processing, rule-based decision systems, preference-aware recommendations, LLM-assisted explanation, lightweight architecture, daily automation, and personalized guidance systems define how the system operates.
